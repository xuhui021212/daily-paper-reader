# LiquidTAD: An Efficient Method for Temporal Action Detection via Liquid Neural Dynamics 

Zepeng Sun , Naichuan Zheng[1] , Hailun Xia , Junjie Wu, Liwei Bao, Xiaotai Zhang 

Abstract —Temporal Action Detection (TAD) in untrimmed videos is currently dominated by Transformer-based architec-tures. While high-performing, their quadratic computational complexity and substantial parameter redundancy limit de-ployment in resource-constrained environments. In this paper, we propose LiquidTAD, a novel parameter-efficient framework that replaces cumbersome self-attention layers with parallelized ActionLiquid blocks. Unlike traditional Liquid Neural Networks (LNNs) that suffer from sequential execution bottlenecks, Liquid-TAD leverages a closed-form continuous-time (CfC) formulation, allowing the model to be reformulated as a parallelizable operator while preserving the intrinsic physical prior of continuous-time dynamics. This architecture captures complex temporal depen-dencies with O(N ) linear complexity and adaptively modulates temporal sensitivity through learned time-constants ( τ ), provid-ing a robust mechanism for handling varying action durations. To the best of our knowledge, this work is the first to introduce a parallelized LNN-based architecture to the TAD domain. Experimental results on the THUMOS-14 dataset demonstrate that LiquidTAD achieves a highly competitive Average mAP of 69.46% with only 10.82M parameters—a 63% reduction compared to the ActionFormer baseline. Further evaluations on ActivityNet-1.3 and Ego4D benchmarks confirm that LiquidTAD achieves an optimal accuracy-efficiency trade-off and exhibits superior robustness to temporal sampling variations, advancing the Pareto frontier of modern TAD frameworks. 

Index Terms —Temporal action detection, liquid neural net-works, closed-form continuous-time, parameter efficiency, Pareto front. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Temporal Action Detection (TAD) is a fundamental task in video signal understanding, aiming to precisely localize and categorize action instances within untrimmed videos [1], [2]. A critical challenge in TAD is capturing complex tem-poral dynamics across diverse action durations, ranging from brief transient movements to prolonged activities. Currently, Transformer-based architectures dominate the landscape [3], [4], relying on self-attention mechanisms to achieve state-of-the-art accuracy. However, the core operator of these frameworks—the standard self-attention block—is inherently over-parameterized, requiring massive linear projections and wide feed-forward networks (FFNs). To model multiscale temporal contexts, these dense blocks must be extensively stacked, creating a formidable “parameter wall”—with leading models often exceeding 30 million parameters—severely re-stricting their scalability and practical deployment in resource-constrained environments.

To dismantle this parameter wall, we break away from con-ventional discrete paradigms and introduce Liquid Neural Net-works (LNNs) [5] to the TAD field for the first time. Through our proposed LiquidTAD framework, we fundamentally re-think temporal modeling as a continuous-time dynamical system . Instead of relying on structurally redundant attention matrices, LiquidTAD formulates temporal aggregation within local windows via an explicit ∆t-aware closed-form update derived from continuous-time dynamics [6]. We introduce the ActionLiquid module, which leverages a parallelized formulation of closed-form liquid dynamics . Unlike many ODE-style liquid models that incur sequential execution or solver overhead, our ActionLiquid block reformulates the state evolution as a parallelizable operator, inheriting the physical prior of continuous modeling while maintaining the training efficiency of modern parallel architectures. Crucially, we deploy a hierarchical liquid architecture 

across the feature pyramid [7]. Instead of using independent, heavy self-attention layers at each level, we utilize lightweight ActionLiquid blocks tailored to each specific temporal scale. This design allows the learned time-constants ( τ ) at different pyramid levels to autonomously adapt to their respective temporal resolutions—capturing rapid, high-frequency signals at lower levels while maintaining long-term dependencies at higher levels. Even with scale-specific parameters, the inherent compactness of the liquid dynamics ensures that LiquidTAD remains significantly more efficient than stacked Transformer baselines, yielding a highly expressive yet compact represen-tation. Extensive experiments on standard benchmarks validate the superiority of our approach. On the THUMOS-14 dataset [2], LiquidTAD achieves a highly competitive Average mAP of 69.46% utilizing merely 10.82 M parameters and 27.17 G FLOPs. Compared to the prominent ActionFormer baseline [3], our framework achieves a 63% reduction in parameters while delivering superior detection accuracy under our experimental protocol , thereby significantly advancing the Pareto frontier of the accuracy-efficiency trade-off in TAD. Furthermore, evaluations on ActivityNet-1.3 [1] and the chal-lenging Ego4D dataset [8] confirm the robust generalization capability and the unique resilience of the liquid dynamics prior to temporal sampling variations. A. Overall Architecture 

The LiquidTAD framework follows a streamlined, single-stage, anchor-free detection paradigm [9], [3]. Given an untrimmed video, we extract frame-level features via a pre-trained backbone, such as I3D [10] or SlowFast [11]. These features are processed by a liquid temporal projection module, comprising a stem liquid encoder and a hierarchical temporal pyramid [7]. At each level l of the pyramid, the sequence is processed by a scale-specific ActionLiquid block to capture dependencies relevant to that resolution. Finally, the multiscale features are fed into a shared anchor-free detection head to output classification scores and temporal boundary regressions. 

B. The ActionLiquid Block: Parallelized Liquid Dynamics 

The core innovation of LiquidTAD is the ActionLiquid block, which replaces structurally redundant self-attention with a parameter-efficient, continuous-time inspired operator [5].

2) Closed-form LTC-style Update: ActionLiquid models the state evolution via an explicit ∆t-aware closed-form update . Inspired by Liquid Time-Constant (LTC) dynamics [5], the update is formulated as:

Physical Interpretation: This formulation adopts a closed-form LTC-style discretization (exponential relaxation form) derived from continuous-time dynamics [6]. The term e−τ ∆t

acts as a retention factor preserving historical context, while 

(1 − e−τ ∆t) represents the innovation rate . By incorporating the physical time-step ∆t directly into the exponent, the model explicitly accounts for the temporal interval between feature indices . This allows the learned τ to adaptively compensate for varying action scales and sampling densities, providing a robust continuous-time prior that discrete attention mechanisms lack. 

C. Complexity and Parameter Efficiency Analysis 

The ActionLiquid block fundamentally dismantles the ”pa-rameter wall” through two key properties:  

> •

Linear Complexity O(CkT ): Given a fixed channel dimension C and kernel size k, the ActionLiquid block processes temporal features through 1D depthwise con-volutions [12] and element-wise exponential updates. The computational complexity remains strictly linear with respect to the video length T , avoiding the quadratic 

O(T 2) bottleneck of standard self-attention mechanisms [14].  

> •

Structural Parsimony: By replacing massive Q/K/V 

projection matrices and wide FFNs with decoupled gating and compact 1D kernels, LiquidTAD achieves extreme parameter economy. Under our experimental setup, our framework requires only 10.82 M parameters and 27.17 G FLOPs for the THUMOS-14 configuration, represent-ing a 63% reduction compared to the ActionFormer baseline [3] while maintaining superior performance. III. E XPERIMENTS 

A. Experimental Setup 

Datasets and Metrics. We comprehensively evaluate Liq-uidTAD on standard Temporal Action Detection (TAD) bench-marks, primarily focusing on THUMOS-14 [2] to assess the model’s capability in localizing dense and short actions. Furthermore, ActivityNet-1.3 [1] and Ego4D [8] are utilized to verify its generalization across long-duration videos and egocentric views. We report the mean Average Precision (mAP) at various temporal Intersection over Union (tIoU) thresholds, along with the average mAP. 

Implementation Details. Following standard practices, we utilize pre-trained visual encoders to extract frame-level fea-tures without fine-tuning. The network is optimized using AdamW [15]. The physical time-step ∆t is strictly aligned with the feature extraction stride. Our framework is imple-mented based on the OpenTAD codebase [ ?] to ensure fair comparisons.

IV. C ONCLUSION 

In this paper, we present LiquidTAD , a parameter-efficient framework that replaces redundant self-attention with paral-lelized ActionLiquid blocks to capture temporal dependencies through a ∆t-aware closed-form LTC-style formulation. By maintaining a strictly linear complexity O(CkT ), LiquidTAD achieves highly competitive performance on THUMOS-14 with only 10.82M parameters—a 63% reduction compared to Transformer-based baselines—while advancing the Pareto frontier across ActivityNet-1.3 and Ego4D. Furthermore, the autonomously learned non-monotonic distribution of time-constants ( τ ) provides the architecture with unique numerical elasticity , enabling it to explicitly offset temporal sampling perturbations by adaptively re-scaling the physical time-step 

∆t. This shift toward compact, continuous-time dynamics of-fers a promising, robust, and interpretable path for high-fidelity video analysis in resource-constrained, real-world scenarios.
