# ABSTRACT 

Leveraging the natural spatiotemporal energy decay in video diffusion offers a path to efficiency, yet relying solely on rigid static masks risks losing critical long-range information in complex dynamics. To address this issue, we propose DynamicRad , a unified sparse-attention paradigm that grounds adaptive selection within a radial locality prior. DynamicRad introduces a dual-mode strategy: 

static-ratio for speed-optimized execution and dynamic-threshold for quality-first filtering. To ensure robustness without online search overhead, we integrate an offline Bayesian Optimization (BO) pipeline coupled with a semantic motion router . This lightweight projection module maps prompt embeddings to optimal sparsity regimes with minimal runtime overhead . Unlike online profiling methods, our offline BO optimizes attention reconstruction error (MSE) on a physics-based proxy task, ensuring rapid convergence. Experiments on HunyuanVideo and Wan2.1-14B demonstrate that DynamicRad pushes the efficiency–quality Pareto frontier, achieving 1.7 ×–2.5 × inference speedups 

with over 80% effective sparsity . In some long-sequence settings, the dynamic mode even matches or exceeds the dense baseline, while mask-aware LoRA further improves long-horizon coherence. Code is available at https://github.com/Adamlong3/DynamicRad .

# 1 Introduction 

Recent diffusion-based video generators achieve strong visual fidelity and temporal coherence, yet scaling them to long horizons and high resolution remains difficult because self-attention grows quadratically with the spatiotemporal token count [Ho et al., 2020, Song et al., 2021, Rombach et al., 2022, Peebles and Xie, 2023, Singer et al., 2022, Hong et al., 2022, Bar-Tal et al., 2024, OpenAI, 2024, Vaswani et al., 2017]. Even with optimized kernels, attention-side compute and memory bandwidth remain major bottlenecks [Dao et al., 2022, Dao, 2024, Bolya et al., 2023, Ma et al., 2024]. A natural remedy is sparsity. Prior efficient attention methods mainly fall into two categories: static sparse patterns, which are hardware-friendly but rigid, and dynamic sparsification, which is adaptive but often introduces extra overhead or instability under heterogeneous and long-video settings [Child et al., 2019, Beltagy et al., 2020, Zaheer et al., 2020, Kitaev et al., 2020, Wang et al., 2020, Choromanski et al., 2021, Roy et al., 2021, Tay et al., 2022]. Recent evidence further suggests a spatiotemporal locality structure in video diffusion attention [Li et al., 2025, Zhang et al., 2025b, Ding et al., 2025], where post-softmax attention mass decays with spatial and temporal distance, motivating sparse masks that preserve near-field interactions while pruning long-range pairs [Li et al., 2025, Zhang et al., 2025b]. However, purely static sparsification remains brittle under complex motion, whereas fully dynamic schemes may be costly or unstable at scale. In this work, we propose DynamicRad , a unified sparse-attention framework for long video diffusion that combines structured sparsity with content adaptivity. # 2 Related Work 

Evolution of Video Generation Backbones. Video generation has shifted from U-Net backbones [Singer et al., 2022] to DiT-style architectures [Peebles and Xie, 2023] due to superior scalability. Representative models such as Latte [Ma et al., 2025], CogVideoX [Yang et al., 2024], VideoPoet [Kondratyuk et al., 2024], and long-context systems [Henschel et al., 2025] extend generation length and quality, but also amplify the attention bottleneck. 

Sparse Attention & Efficiency Paradigms. Scaling diffusion models to long horizons is hindered by the quadratic 

O(N 2) cost of attention [Vaswani et al., 2017, Dao et al., 2022]. Prior works typically bifurcate into static patterns 

that impose fixed locality priors [Child et al., 2019, Li et al., 2025] and dynamic routing that adapts to content [Roy et al., 2021, Xi et al., 2025, Zhang et al., 2025b]. While Radial Attention [Li et al., 2025] is hardware-friendly, it lacks adaptivity. Conversely, SVG [Xi et al., 2025] enables dynamic routing but adds online overhead. To address these gaps, recent works have introduced novel structural efficiencies. Dynamic Diffusion Transformer (DyDiT) [Zhao et al., 2024] dynamically adjusts computation in diffusion transformers along timestep and spatial dimensions, while training-free baselines such as PowerAttention (PA) [Chen et al., 2025], SVG [Xi et al., 2025], and 

Radial Attention [Li et al., 2025] improve efficiency from different design perspectives. 

Learning-based Efficiency. Recent approaches like VORTA [Sun et al., 2025] and VSA [Zhang et al., 2025a] propose learning sparsity policies directly via gating networks. While effective, they require expensive retraining or per-step routing inference. In contrast, DynamicRad achieves adaptivity in a training-free manner (for the attention mechanism itself) by leveraging physical motion priors and reusing pre-computed text embeddings, avoiding the need for invasive architectural changes. 

Deployment Optimization Strategies. Our work integrates several orthogonal efficiency techniques. Similar to TeaCache [Liu et al., 2024] and DeepCache [Ma et al., 2024] which skip layers to save compute, we reduce the intra-layer attention cost. To ensure temporal coherence under aggressive pruning, we employ Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) akin to LoRA [Hu et al., 2022], but specifically target projection layers sensitive to sparsity. Finally, instead of costly online neural architecture search (NAS) [Snoek et al., 2012], we utilize an offline Bayesian Optimization pipeline to pre-compile a lightweight lookup table, enabling instantaneous adaptation at runtime. 

# 3 Method 

Radial Attention reduces attention cost via a static energy-decay mask [Li et al., 2025], but its fixed sparsity pattern can be brittle under long horizons and complex motion where long-range dependencies become critical. To resolve these limitations, we propose DynamicRad , a unified sparse-attention paradigm summarized in Figure 1. The framework combines offline BO-based configuration, prompt-conditioned regime routing, and a shared candidate set with two selection modes: static-ratio for throughput-first deployment and dynamic-threshold for quality-first deployment. An optional mask-aware LoRA further compensates for sparsity-induced degradation. 3.4 Automated Configuration: Semantic-Aware Motion Routing 

Since text prompts inherently encode semantic motion priors (e.g., ’reading’ vs. ’running’) [Wang et al., 2024, Guo et al., 2024], we leverage the prompt embedding to predict the global motion regime. the motion dynamics implied by the text prompt. Naive heuristic rules often fail to capture semantic nuances (e.g., “a fast snail” implies low motion despite the word “fast”)[Wu et al., 2023]. This ensures that DynamicRad can be rapidly adapted to new resolutions or aspect ratios. 

Architecture and Inference. Since modern video diffusion models (e.g., HunyuanVideo) utilize heavy text encoders (e.g., LLM or CLIP) for conditioning, we can reuse the pre-computed text embeddings etext to guide sparsity. We append a lightweight 2-layer MLP projection head ϕ(·) to predict a motion intensity score: 

smotion = ϕ(Pool (etext )) ∈ [0 , 1] . (15) This score is discretized to select the motion regime c ∈ { Low , Mid , High } (e.g., s < 0.3 → Low ), which indexes the offline BO lookup table. Crucially, this router introduces negligible overhead . Since etext is pre-computed, the router implies a single MLP pass taking <2ms, negligible compared to the generation latency. At inference time, we encode the prompt, predict a motion regime, retrieve the corresponding sparsity configuration from the lookup table, and run generation with the selected mode. 

Training with Optical Flow Proxy. To supervise ϕ(·) without expensive human annotation, we employ a physics-grounded proxy task. We construct a dataset Dtrain from a subset of OpenVid-1M [Nan et al., 2025]. For each video-text pair, we calculate the average optical flow magnitude between adjacent frames as the ground-truth motion label yflow .The projection head is trained to minimize the regression loss L = ∥smotion − Norm (yflow )∥22. This aligns the semantic understanding of the router with the physical motion priors used in our offline BO optimization.

4.1 Experimental Setup Implementation & Hardware. We implement DynamicRad using FlashInfer kernels ( Bs = 32 ) on NVIDIA H100 GPUs . Standard inference uses a single H100, while HunyuanVideo-241f ( >200k tokens) employs an 8×H100 cluster 

(sequence parallelism) to prevent OOM. The Semantic Motion Router is frozen during inference; its parameter search follows the offline BO pipeline (Sec. 3.4). Mask-Aware LoRA ( r = 16 ) is fine-tuned for 1k steps on a subset of OpenVid-1M using the standard diffusion denoising loss under the selected sparse mask, while keeping the backbone frozen. Detailed hyperparameters, overhead quantification, and proxy-task analysis are provided in the supplementary material.

4.2 Main Results Efficiency Dominance. Table 1 demonstrates that DynamicRad significantly advances the efficiency–quality Pareto frontier. Our static-ratio mode establishes a new throughput ceiling. On HunyuanVideo-241f, it achieves a 2.54 ×

speedup (324s), surpassing Radial Attention (351s). Similarly, on Wan2.1-14B (145f), we achieve a 2.25 × speedup 

(vs. Radial Attention’s 2.02 ×), effectively halving the inference time compared to the dense baseline. 

Quality & "Denoising" Effect. The dynamic-threshold mode proves sparsity need not compromise fidelity. On HunyuanVideo-241f, it outperforms even the dense baseline (VisionReward 0.131 vs. 0.116). We hypothesize that this is partly attributable to the noise-filtering property of our semantic routing, which prevents "quality collapse" in long sequences by pruning irrelevant OOD tokens. 

Robustness Across Architectures. The gains are consistent across the DiT-based HunyuanVideo and the VAE-based Wan2.1. On Wan2.1-14B (72f), our static mode maintains a 1.92 × speedup with 78.8% sparsity , while the dynamic mode is on par with the dense model’s temporal consistency (VBench S.C. 0.959 vs. 0.958), verifying that our Router-guided paradigm generalizes effectively to different spatiotemporal tokenization strategies.

We presented DynamicRad , a unified paradigm reconciling kernel-friendly sparsity with content adaptivity for long video diffusion. By implementing a dual-mode strategy via an offline BO-driven router, DynamicRad achieves 1.7 ×–2.5 × speedups with >80% sparsity on HunyuanVideo and Wan2.1-14B, significantly advancing inference efficiency. Crucially, we demonstrated that a lightweight mask-aware LoRA preserves temporal coherence under aggressive pruning.

(3) Hardware Generalization: 

Because the method relies on specific block sizes ( Bs), non-server GPUs may require local tuning and LUT recalibration.
