# Abstract 

As a classic vision task, anomaly detection has been widely applied in industrial inspection and medi-cal imaging. In this task, data scarcity is often a frequently-faced issue. To solve it, the few-shot anomaly detection (FSAD) scheme is attracting in-creasing attention. In recent years, beyond tra-ditional visual paradigm, Vision-Language Model (VLM) has been extensively explored to boost this field. However, in currently-existing VLM-based FSAD schemes, almost all perform anomaly infer-ence only by pairwise feature matching, ignoring structural dependencies and global consistency. To further redound to FSAD via VLM, we propose a 

Heterogeneous Hypergraph Vision-Language Rea-soning (H2VLR ) framework. It re-formulates the FSAD as a high-order inference problem of visual-semantic relations, by jointly modeling visual re-gions and semantic concepts in a unified hyper-graph. Experimental comparisons verify the ef-fectiveness and advantages of H 2VLR. It could of-ten achieve state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance on representative industrial and medical benchmarks. Our code will be released upon acceptance. 

# 1 Introduction 

Anomaly detection is a fundamental problem in computer vi-sion, aiming to identify deviations from normal patterns in visual data. It plays a vital role in safety-critical applications such as industrial inspection and medical imaging, where un-detected anomalies may lead to severe economic loss or clini-cal risk [Li et al. , 2024b]. In these domains, reliable anomaly detection is essential for ensuring system robustness and de-cision safety. In practice, visual anomaly detection is severely con-strained by the scarcity of anomalous samples. Anomalies are inherently rare, diverse, and often unpredictable, making it costly and impractical to collect sufficient training data [Gu 

et al. , 2025]. As a result, conventional data-driven anomaly detection methods struggle to generalize under limited su-pervision [Gao et al. , 2026]. These challenges have driven

(II) We design a group of semantic inducing and hyper-graph modeling schemes for H 2VLR. (III) We design a high-order relation reasoning strategy on visual-semantic feature hypergraph for the effective detection and location inference of image anomaly regions. 

# 2 Related Work 

FSAD aims to characterize normality from extremely limited reference samples and identify deviations at inference time. Early FSAD primarily follows a reconstruction paradigm (e.g. , MemAE [Gong et al. , 2019], HTDG [Sheynin et al. ,2021]) to learn normal appearance from few references, while later works shift to feature-matching and memory-augmented paradigms ( e.g. , PaDiM [Defard et al. , 2021], PatchCore [Roth et al. , 2022]) that detect anomalies through nearest-neighbor retrieval in pretrained representations. Recently, many efforts have explored enhanced general-ization through transferable learning paradigms. Some meta learning-based methods, such as RegAD [Huang et al. , 2022], enable category-agnostic adaptation through learned compar-ison mechanisms. Moreover, some Vision-Language mod-els, such as WinCLIP [Jeong et al. , 2023] and Anomaly-CLIP [Zhou et al. , 2023], introduce semantic supervision through cross-modal alignment. In parallel, some graph-enhanced and diffusion-based models ( e.g. , DiffAD [Zhang 

et al. , 2023]) incorporate structural cues or generative priors to better model normal distributions. 

VLMs enable semantic-aware FSAD by introducing text as a supervisory signal, alleviating the need for category-specific training. PromptAD [Li et al. , 2024a] leverages tex-tual prompts to condition anomaly scoring, improving cross-category transfer via visual-semantic alignment. Moreover, DictAS [Qu et al. , 2025] formulates class-generalizable few-shot anomaly segmentation as dictionary lookup from a few normal references, without target-domain retraining. However, existing approaches such as AnomalyCLIP and AdaptCLIP [Zhou et al. , 2023; Gao et al. , 2026] rely on tra-ditional similarity matching between visual and textual repre-sentations, preventing the consistency of abnormal structures, for lacking explicit high-order relational modeling. 

Hypergraph learning generalizes traditional graphs by al-lowing hyper-edges to connect multiple nodes, effectively capturing high-order correlations beyond simplistic pairwise constraints. For example, the early research Learning with Hypergraph [Zhou et al. , 2006] establishes a unified spectral learning framework that enables clustering and classification directly on hypergraph structures. The recent advances in geometric deep learning, e.g. ,HGNN [Feng et al. , 2018], HyperGCN [Yadati et al. , 2019], and unified frameworks like UniGNN [Huang and Yang, 2021] or AllSet [Chien et al. , 2022], have enabled efficient message-passing for complex structural learning. In com-puter vision, some paradigms like HgVT [Fixelle, 2025] and HSL [Cai et al. , 2022] further leverage hypergraphs to model spatial-semantic dependencies. Currently, most hypergraph learning approaches focus on single-modality representations in supervised or closed-set scenarios, with a limited appli-cability to anomaly detection under few-shot conditions. In particular, how to utilize hypergraph-based reasoning to cap-ture the cross-modal interactions between visual regions and textual prompts still remains largely unexplored.

3.2 Method Overview 

As shown in Fig. 2, we propose the H 2VLR, a unified cross-modal hypergraph framework for few-shot anomaly detec-tion. Instead of relying on independent image–text similarity estimation, it explicitly models the high-order interactions be-tween adaptive textual semantics and multi-scale visual struc-tures. Firstly, a heterogeneous semantic-guided hypergraph is constructed to embed visual patches and dynamically-generated textual prompts into a shared relational space, where hyperedges encode cross-modal and structural depen-dencies. A hypergraph message passing mechanism is then applied to perform semantic-constrained relational reasoning, enabling structurally meaningful deviations to be emphasized while suppressing unreliable responses. Finally, the refined relational features are incorporated into anomaly inference to yield noise-robust image-level scores and pixel-level precise localization. This formulation provides a compact yet prin-cipled pathway from adaptive semantics to high-order struc-tural reasoning and reliable anomaly decision. It generates the final prediction by fusing a coarse Base Map from the Semantic Alignment Branch (SAB) with a structure-aware Residual Map from the Hypergraph Reasoning Branch (HRB), effectively synergizing the global semantic priors with high-order topological constraints.

3.4 High-order Reasoning for Anomaly 

Based on the heterogeneous incidence structure H and the prompt-induced edge weights θ, a high-order reasoning mod-ule is designed to explicitly infer anomalies as the viola-tions of semantic–structural consistency, rather than tradi-tional patch-level deviations. In few-shot scenarios, local appearance variations are often ambiguous, making a direct similarity-based decision unreliable. Hypergraph reasoning addresses this limitation by propagating anomaly evidence across semantically aligned and structurally coherent regions, enabling context-aware anomaly inference. Given a query image Q, we extract patch tokens PG by the frozen image encoder Eimage , and compute an init anomaly estimation Mbase through the Semantic Alignment Branch (SAB). Specifically, a text-driven anomaly map Mtxt is ob-tained by the cosine similarity between query patches and the induced semantic repository R. Meanwhile, a visual-driven map Mvis is computed via the KNN matching with the sup-port feature gallery. These two complementary cues are com-bined together to form the base anomaly map

# 4 Experiments 

4.1 Settings 

Datasets. We comprehensively evaluate H 2VLR on 5 in-dustrial benchmarks (MVTec [Bergmann et al. , 2019], VisA [Zou et al. , 2022], BTAD [Mishra et al. , 2021], MPDD [Jezek 

et al. , 2021], self-constructed BeltAD) and 3 medical datasets (BrainMRI [Salehi et al. , 2021], LiverCT [Bao et al. , 2024], BUSI [Al-Dhabyani et al. , 2020]). 

Evaluation Metrics. Following the common practice in anomaly detection, we report the Area Under the ROC Curve at both image level (I-AUC) and pixel level (P-AUC), to eval-uate detection and localization performance, respectively. 

Implementation Details. H2VLR is implemented in Py-Torch with a frozen OpenCLIP (ViT-B/16+) backbone [Il-harco et al. , 2021]. Optimization is performed by SGD for 100 epochs ( lr 2 × 10 −3, batch size 400). The image res-olution for training and evaluation is 240 × 240 . Objective weights are configured as λstr 0.02 and λseg 1.0. The hy-pergraph is with K = 8 and L = 2 . All experiments are conducted on a single NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPU.

# 5 Conclusions 

In this work, we propose the H 2VLR, a Heterogeneous hyper-graph Vision-Language Reasoning framework for few-shot anomaly detection. Beyond traditional pairwise matching strategies, it could explicitly models the high-order relations of visual-semantic anomaly features on a unified hypergraph. This new strategy enables our method to effectively capture the global structural consistency and spatial–semantic depen-dencies of anomaly features. The experimental evaluations on eight industrial or medical benchmarks demonstrate that our H 2VLR framework with semantic inducing, hypergraph modeling and high-order reasoning is effective. It could of-ten consistently achieves SOTA detection performance in the real-world few-shot learning scenarios of anomaly detection. In the future, more complex scenario adaptivity ( e.g. , 3D anomaly) of our method is worth of exploration. References
