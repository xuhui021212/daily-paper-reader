Abstract. Retinal diagnosis is inherently bilateral: clinicians compare homologous structures across eyes (e.g., optic disc asymmetry), yet most deep models operate on monocular representations. We investi-gate whether explicit structural correspondence improves diagnosis, and propose Anatomy-Slot to operationalize this hypothesis. Anatomy-Slot introduces an unsupervised anatomical bottleneck by decomposing patch tokens into slots and aligning slots across eyes via bidirectional cross-attention. On ODIR-5K with n = 10 seeds, the method improves AUC by 4.2% over a matched ViT-L baseline (95% CIs; Wilcoxon signed-rank test, W = 0 , p = 0 .002 ). Pairing disruption and stress testing under Gaussian noise provide controlled tests of correspondence dependence and robustness under corruption. We further report quantitative optic disc grounding on REFUGE and cross-attention localization analysis. 

Keywords: Bilateral reasoning · Slot Attention · Retinal diagnosis ·

Unsupervised anatomical factorization · Interpretability. 

## 1 Introduction 

Clinical retinal assessment often relies on comparing homologous structures across left and right eyes. We hypothesize that texture-based monocular classification is insufficient for bilateral disease detection, and that explicit structural comparison is necessary for clinically aligned performance. We predict that (i) models with explicit correspondence should outperform matched monocular baselines, and (ii) their performance should degrade when correspondence is disrupted. We further predict that neither bilateral processing without structured correspondence units nor slot decomposition without cross-eye interaction will achieve the full gain. While bilateral modeling and slot-based decomposition exist independently, their integration as explicit correspondence units for homologous anatomical reasoning has not been empirically tested under statistically controlled conditions. We present Anatomy-Slot , which decomposes each eye into object-centric slots and aligns slots across eyes using cross-attention. We evaluate this design under matched-backbone settings and report controlled ablations and mechanistic

## 2 Related Works 

Bilateral retinal analysis has been explored with Siamese or dual-branch archi-tectures for fundus and OCT tasks, often enforcing consistency, symmetry, or cross-eye alignment constraints and aggregating paired evidence through late fusion or correspondence-aware heads [24, 19, 14, 20]. These approaches typically focus on paired prediction or feature fusion but do not explicitly represent corre-spondence with object-centric units, making it difficult to test whether models rely on homologous anatomical matching. Medical foundation models, enabled by self-supervised pretraining, have be-come a central paradigm for retinal diagnosis and broader clinical imaging. Masked image modeling (MAE), self-distillation (DINO, DINOv2), and contrastive learn-ing (BYOL) with scalable ViT/Swin backbones provide the technical basis for strong representations [8, 3, 15, 7, 5, 12]. Retinal-specific models such as RET-Found demonstrate clear gains on ocular and systemic prediction tasks [25]. Object-centric learning (e.g., IODINE, MONet, Slot Attention) was developed primarily for natural images to induce compositional scene representations [6, 2, 13]. Our work differs by integrating slot-based decomposition with explicit cross-eye correspondence and evaluating this integration under statistically controlled conditions, with mechanistic tests that probe correspondence dependence.

## 4 Experiments and Analyses 

4.1 Setup 

Datasets. ODIR-5K [17] (bilateral multi-label: N, D, G, C, A, H, M, O). Unlabeled pretraining pool includes ODIR, APTOS2019 [10], IDRiD [18], MESSIDOR2 [1], PAPILA [11], and Retina [9] (total ~20k). External validation on EyePACS [4] (5,311 patients) and anatomical grounding on REFUGE (validation set). 

Backbone and training. RETFound ViT-L/16 [25]. For pretraining we unfreeze the last block and the layer normalization parameters; for main training we unfreeze block 23 and the layer normalization parameters. Slot Attention iterations: 5 (pretraining) and 3 (main training). Slots: K = 8 by default. 

Statistics. We run n = 10 independent seeds and report mean μ, standard deviation σ, and 95% CIs. Since Shapiro–Wilk tests did not indicate normality, paired comparisons use the Wilcoxon signed-rank test. 4.2 Main Results, Ablations, and Capacity 

Main results and ablations are summarized in Table 1. The ablation pattern indicates that bilateral processing without slots yields a modest gain (0.828 vs. 0.823 AUC), while slots without bilateral interaction underperform (0.754 AUC). The full model reaches 0.865 AUC, suggesting that the primary improvement arises from the combination of structured slots and cross-eye alignment. The pattern suggests an interaction effect: bilateral processing alone yields limited gain, and slot decomposition without cross-eye interaction underperforms, while their combination produces a substantially larger improvement. The No Slots 

baseline pools patch tokens per eye into a single global token before cross-eye interaction. Without reconstruction, slot representations converge to similar pat-terns (collapse), degrading AUC to 0.729. Fig. 2 summarizes structural necessity and capacity: the full model dominates single-factor variants, and capacity peaks at K = 8 .Adding the reconstruction loss to the bilateral no-slot baseline yields 0.834 AUC (n=10 mean), indicating that reconstruction alone does not explain the full gain of Anatomy-Slot (0.865).

Under the correspondence hypothesis, disrupting left–right pairing should reduce performance because homologous comparison becomes unavailable. The observed 18.3-point drop supports this prediction and serves as a necessary (but not sufficient) condition for correspondence reliance; it does not uniquely identify structural reasoning, as sensitivity to paired input statistics may also contribute. The baseline model trained under identical pairing conditions degrades by only 3.2 points, suggesting the effect is architecture-specific rather than a trivial consequence of paired inputs.

## 5 Discussion and Conclusion 

Anatomy-Slot introduces an unsupervised anatomical bottleneck with explicit bilateral correspondence modeling. Under matched-backbone settings and robust statistical testing, it improves performance and exhibits correspondence-sensitive behavior consistent with bilateral reasoning. Quantitative optic disc grounding and attention localization further support correspondence alignment. Limitations include evaluation on a single primary dataset, partially verified anatomical coverage, and untested tolerance to clinical misalignment. These results suggest a path toward clinically aligned retinal AI and interpretable diagnostic support systems, with broader anatomical validation and clinical misalignment testing as immediate next steps.
